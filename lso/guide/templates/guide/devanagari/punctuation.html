English has a variety of different punctuation marks. When writing Sanskrit, Devanagari has only three:

    {{ row("| || '") }}

The first is the {{ 'daNDa'|i('dfn') }}, or "stick." This marks the end of a sentence or half-verse. The second can be called a double {{ 'daNDa'|i }}. This marks the end of a paragraph or verse. The third is the {{ 'avagraha'|i('dfn') }}. This is sometimes used to indicate a vowel sound that is removed due to blending. The {{ 'avagraha'|i }} is discussed in more detail in the grammar guide.
