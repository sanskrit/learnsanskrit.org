Review ------ We have seen five cases so far. Each corresponds to a different role: !!! examples - {{ m.x('gajaz carati', 'The elephant walks.') }} - {{ m.x('gajaM pazyAmi', 'I see the elephant.') }} - {{ m.x('gajair gacchanti', 'They go by means of the elephants.') }} - {{ m.x('gajAya gacchanti', 'They go for the elephant.') }} - {{ m.x('gajAd gacchanti', 'They go from the elephant.') }} Nouns to nouns -------------- So far, each case has connected a noun to some action. Case 6 does not. Instead, case 6 *connects a noun to another noun.* This connection is abstract and can mean almost anything. But generally, we can translate it with the English word "of": !!! examples - {{ m.x('[narasya] putro jAyate', 'The son [of the man] is born.') }} - {{ m.x('gajaM tudanti zarA [vIrayoH]', 'The arrows [of the two heroes] strike the elephant.') }} - {{ m.x('[bAlAnAM] rathAn gacchati', "He goes to the boys' chariots. (He goes to the chariots [of] the boys)") }} Case 6 is one of the most common Sanskrit cases, and you'll see it frequently.
