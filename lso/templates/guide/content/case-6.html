Case 6 does not. Instead, case 6 *connects a noun to another noun.* This connection is abstract and can mean almost anything: !!! examples - {{ m.x('[narasya] putro jAyate', 'The son [of the man] is born.') }} - {{ m.x('gajaM tudanti zarA [vIrayoH]', 'The arrows [of the two heroes] strike the elephant.') }} - {{ m.x('[bAlAnAm] rathA gacchanti', 'The chariots [of the boys] go.') }} Case 6 is one of the most common Sanskrit cases, and you'll see it frequently.
