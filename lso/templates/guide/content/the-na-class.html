`-nA` and `-nI` --------------- In this class, we form the strong stem by adding `-nA` and the weak stem by adding `-nI`: !!! examples - {{ m.x('krI -> krINAti', 'He buys.') }} - {{ m.x('krI -> krINImaH', 'We buy.') }} Let's call this class the `nA` class. Strong and weak stems --------------------- `krI` uses both `parasmaipada` and `Atmanepada` endings. Here are its forms: ### `parasmaipada` {% include('guide/include/kri-9p.html') %} Note that it's `krINanti`, not `*krINyanti`. The final `-I` disappears in front of vowels. ### `Atmanepada` {% include('guide/include/kri-9a.html') %} Again, the final `-I` disappears in front of vowels. `jJA` ----- The `nA` class has a few irregular verbs, but the most important one is `jJA` ("know"). Its strong stem is `jAnA`, and its weak stem is `jAnI`: !!! examples - {{ m.x('na jAnAmi', "I don't know.") }}
