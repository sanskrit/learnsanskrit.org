`a-` ---- {# 6.3.73-74, or Kale 218-9 #} It's useful to say what something is *not*. In Sanskrit, the easiest way to do this is to stick `a-` to some word: !!! examples - {{ m.x('zokaH -> [azokaH]', 'sorrow -> "un-sorrow", sorrowless') }} But if the word starts with a vowel, we use `an-` instead: !!! examples - {{ m.x('antaH -> [anantaH]', 'end -> unending, limitless') }} !!! note This `a-` has cousins in many other languages. Consider: "a-moral", "an-archy", "in-appropriate", or "un-able." `sa-` ----- {# 6.3.78-84, or Kale 294-5 #} `sa-` is a shorter form of the words `saha` or `samAna`. `saha` is an uninflected word that means "with": !!! examples - {{ m.x('gajena saha -> [sagajaH]', 'with an elephant') }} - {{ m.x('visRjya [sazaraM] cApam', 'after throwing aside his bow [with its arrow]') }} Bhagavad Gita 1.47 And `samAna` is an adjective that means "the same": !!! examples - {{ m.x('yasya samAno garbhaH -> [sagarbhaH]', 'one who has the same womb; sibling, brother') }}
