(case 5)') }} - {{ m.x('grAme tiSThasi', 'You stand in the village. (case 7)') }} One case connects a noun to another noun: !!! examples - {{ m.x('narasya gajam pazyAmaH', "We see the man's elephant. But for the sake of practice, learn to recite these forms in order, from `gajaH` and `gajau` in case 1 all the way to `gajAH` in case 8. Doing so will fix these forms in your mind. ### Ambiguities Tables are not a fun way to study Sanskrit, but they can make certain trends very clear. In the table above, forms with the same background color (except for white) are identical. As always, we should rely on context and common sense to help us solve these ambiguities. Note that some of these ambiguities occur because of how sounds blend: !!! examples - {{ m.x('nara icchati', 'The man wants. (likely)
He wants in the man. (unlikely)') }} Common cases ------------ How often are the different noun cases used? Which are the most important ones to know? This answer can vary from text to text. But in the Bhagavad Gita, the results are quite clear: Of course, you should know the basics of each of the eight cases. But these cases all have subtleties and extra meanings, and it is difficult to study them all at once. So as you continue to study Sanskrit, whether here or elsewhere, be sure to focus on common concepts first!
