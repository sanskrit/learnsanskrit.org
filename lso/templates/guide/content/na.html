A small word ... and a small lesson ----------------------------------- {{ 'na'|i('dfn') }} is an uninflected word that means "no" or "not". It usually goes in front of the verb: !!! examples - {{ m.x('[na] gacchati', 'He [does not] go.') }} In verbless sentences, `na` usually appears next to the noun that's being denied: !!! examples - {{ m.x('naro vIro [na]', 'The man [is not] a hero.') }} - {{ m.x('naro [na] vIraH', 'The man [is not] a hero.') }} But note the subtle difference if we rearrange the sentence slightly: !!! examples - {{ m.x('[na] naro vIraH', 'The hero [is not] a man.') }}
