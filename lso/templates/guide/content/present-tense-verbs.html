We have already seen some simple Sanskrit verbs: !!! examples - {{ m.x('tiSThati', 'He stands.') }} - {{ m.x('pazyataH', 'The two of them see.') }} - {{ m.x('gacchanti', 'They go.') }} These verbs let us describe what is happening **right now**. In English, such verbs are called present tense verbs. But these Sanskrit verbs have a broader meaning than their English counterparts: !!! examples - {{ m.x('tiSThati', 'He is standing.') }} - {{ m.x('pazyataH', 'The two of them are seeing.') }} - {{ m.x('gacchanti', 'They are going.') }} And they have many others too. As much as possible, we should focus on *Sanskrit* words and sentences, not their counterparts in English. The more we rely on English, the less we learn about Sanskrit itself.
