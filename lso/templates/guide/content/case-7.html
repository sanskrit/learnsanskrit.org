Review ------ We have seen six cases so far. Five connect a noun to some action: !!! examples - {{ m.x('gajaz carati', 'The elephant walks.') }} - {{ m.x('gajaM pazyAmi', 'I see the elephant.') }} - {{ m.x('gajair gacchanti', 'They go by means of the elephants.') }} - {{ m.x('gajAya gacchanti', 'They go for the elephant.') }} - {{ m.x('gajAd gacchanti', 'They go from the elephant.') }} and the sixth connects a noun to another noun: !!! examples - {{ m.x('bAlasya gajaz carati', "The boy's elephant walks.") }} "in" ---- Our next case, which we can call case 7, has several different roles that aren't worth analyzing in detail. Simply, we can say that it has the same meaning as the English word "in." !!! examples - {{ m.x('[loke] caranti mRgAH', 'The deer roam in the world.') }} - {{ m.x('bhavanti siMhA [grAmayoH]', 'Lions are in the two villages.') }} - {{ m.x('[gRheSu] jAyante putrAH', 'Sons are born in the houses.') }} As usual, we can use multiple cases at once: !!! examples - {{ m.x('gRhe devaM pazyAmi', 'I see the god in the house.') }} - {{ m.x('loke narA bhavanti', 'The men are in the world.') }} - {{ m.x('grAme devAH putrAMz corayanti', 'The gods steal the sons in the village.') }}
{% filter markdown %} ## Blending `-e` The letter `e` behaves simply enough: !!! examples - {{ m.x('gRhe pacadhve', 'You all cook for yourselves in the house') }} - {{ m.x('gRhe jAyete putrau', "The two sons are born in the house.") }} - {{ m.x('gRhe manyAmahe', 'We think in the house.') }} Still, what happens when `e` is near vowels? This, too, is familiar to us already: a sentence like `grAme icchanti` will become `grAmayicchanti`. But when this blending occurred between two words, Sanskrit speakers went one step further. They thought `y` was a tiresome letter to have to say between these two words, so they removed it entirely: !!! examples - {{ m.x('grAme icchanti -> grAma icchanti', 'They want in the village.') }} But consider a phrase like `kRSNaH azvaH`, which becomes `kRSNo 'zvaH`. `aH` overwhelms the `a`, and `e` does the same. !!! examples - {{ m.x("gRhe azvaH bhavati -> gRhe 'zvo bhavati", 'The horse is in the house.') }} This change is unusual and against our Sanskrit intuition. But it is the sort of change that we must just accept. {% endfilter %}
