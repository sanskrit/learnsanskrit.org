Review ------ There's a basic set of endings that we can use for all sorts of new stems. For example, here are the endings for `marut`: {% include 'guide/include/marut-m.html' %} Multiple stems -------------- A few consonants, like `marut`, are small and simple. But most consonant stems are made by adding stem suffixes to other words: These suffixes actually use *multiple stems*. Sometimes the stems are strong: !!! examples - {{ m.x('bhagavat -> bhaga[vantaH]', 'The blessed ones (case 1)') }} - {{ m.x('bhagavat -> bhaga[vantam]', 'The blessed one (case 2)') }} And sometimes they're weak: !!! examples - {{ m.x('bhagavat -> bhaga[vataH]', 'The blessed ones (case 2)') }} - {{ m.x('bhagavat -> bhaga[vatsu]', 'Among the blessed ones (case 7)') }} Do you see the difference? Here the strong stem uses `-vant` and the weak stem uses `-vat`. I do not know why this change occurs and have no good explanation for it. But the good news is that the endings are the same as before. The next few lessons will introduce some of these special suffixes.
