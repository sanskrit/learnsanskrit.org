Review ------ There's a basic set of endings that we can use for all sorts of new stems. For example, here are the endings for `marut`: {% include 'guide/include/marut-m.html' %} Multiple stems -------------- A few consonants, like `marut`, are small and simple. But most consonant stems are made by adding suffixes to other words: Many of these suffixes actually use *multiple stems*. They have a strong stem that's used in certain places: !!! examples - {{ m.x('Atman -> At[mAnam]', 'The self (case 2)') }} Otherwise, they use a weak stem in front of consonants: !!! examples - {{ m.x('Atman -> Atma[su]', 'In the selves') }} An an unchanged stem in front of vowels: !!! examples - {{ m.x('Atman -> AtmanaH', 'Of the self') }} The next few lessons will make all of this concrete, so don't worry about the details here. Why have multiple stems? ------------------------ Weak stems make the full word a little easier to pronounce, but I don't know why strong stems exist and have no explanation for them. However, I *do* have two pieces of good news. First, all of the endings we use are still the same. And second, this strong/weak stem business is used only in the *masculine* gender.
