{% from 'guide/macros-devanagari.html' import rows %} English has a variety of different punctuation marks. Sanskrit uses only three: {{ rows("| || '") }} The first is the `daNDa`, or "stick." This marks the end of a sentence or half-verse. The second can be called a double `daNDa`. This marks the end of a paragraph or full verse. The third is the `avagraha`. This is used mainly to indicate that a vowel sound was removed due to blending. !!! note The grammar guide discusses the `avagraha` in more detail.
