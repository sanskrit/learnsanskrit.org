Three root suffixes ------------------- `-an`, `-man`, and `-van` are *root* suffixes. They don't cause a clear change in meaning. But `-man` tends to create neuter abstract nouns. Here are some examples you might recognize: !!! examples - {{ m.x('kR + man -> karma', 'do -> the act of doing; karma') }} - {{ m.x('jan + man -> janma', 'be born -> the act of being born; birth') }} - {{ m.x('bRh + man -> brahma', 'grow, increase, expand -> Brahman (neuter)') }} But this isn't universal: !!! examples - {{ m.x('bRh + man -> brahmA', 'grow, increase, expand -> Brahma, the creator (masculine)') }} Endings ------- ### Masculine The endings are the same as before. The strong stems end in `-An/-mAn/-vAn`, the normal stems end in `-an/-man/-van`, and the weak stem ends in `-a/-ma/-va`: {% include 'guide/include/atman-m.html' %} Here, the **strong** stems have black borders. We see that the strong stems are used in cases 1, 2, and 8. But when case 2 refers to many things, the weak stem is used instead. ### Neuter As usual, everything here is weak: {% include 'guide/include/karman-n.html' %} ### Feminine The feminine endings are `-anI`, `-manI`, and `-vanI`. These follow the pattern of all other stems that end in `-I`. We'll see how these stems work in a later lesson. Contraction ----------- Certain stems will *contract*: !!! examples - {{ m.x('dhAmani -> dhAmni', 'in the house') }} - {{ m.x('rAjane -> rAjJe', 'for the king') }} The pattern here is tricky. Basically, we contract the stem if the part before `-an` is a consonant cluster ending in `-m` or `-v`. Therefore, these stems don't contract: !!! examples - {{ m.x('Atmane', 'for the self') }} - {{ m.x('atharvaNi', 'in the Atharva Veda') }}
