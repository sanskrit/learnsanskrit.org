Review ------ We can use *pronouns* to refer to nouns in general: !!! examples - {{ m.x('naro gacchati -> sa gacchati', 'The man goes. -> He goes.') }} Pronominal adjectives --------------------- There are a few adjectives that use the same endings that the pronouns do. We can call them pronominal adjectives. Instead of listing them exhaustively, I'll just give a few examples: !!! examples - {{ m.x('[sarve] yuddhavizAradAH', '[They all] are skilled in battle.') }} Bhagavad Gita 1.6 - {{ m.x('[anye] ca bahavaH zUrAH', 'And many [other] warriors.') }} Bhagavad Gita 1.9 Traditional grammar doesn't really distinguish between pronouns and pronominal adjectives. They are all simply called `sarva-nAman` ("name for all", a `tatpuruSa` compound).
