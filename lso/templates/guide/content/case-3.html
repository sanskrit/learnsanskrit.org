{% filter markdown %} ## Blending `n` inside a word Inside a single word, `n` sometimes changes to `N`: !!! examples - {{ m.x('narena -> nareNa', 'with the man') }} This change is often explained in a complex way. But there's a simple explanation too. When you pronounce `r`, your tongue moves toward the back of your mouth. (If your tongue doesn't move this way, it should!) The tongue sits coiled like a cobra waiting to strike. And `n` is its prey. If a sound appears that *forces the tongue to move out of position*, the tongue resets, and the `n` survives another day. These sounds include `c`, `dh`, `l`, and many others, but *not* sounds like `gh` or `p` or `h` or `y`. But if nothing distracting comes along, the coiled-up tongue can pounce: !!! examples - {{ m.x('rAmAyana -> rAmAyaNa') }} - {{ m.x('rAvana -> rAvaNa') }} - {{ m.x('nagarena -> nagareNa') }} That's all.
