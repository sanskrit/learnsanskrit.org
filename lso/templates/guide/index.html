Sanskrit guide

This is the new version of the grammar guide. It's still under development. To see the old guide, go to old.learnsanskrit.org.

You can follow the guide's progress by subscribing to the mailing list.

Part 1: Foundations

After reflecting on why we want to learn Sanskrit and learning how the guide works, we dive into the foundations of the Sanskrit language. Once we understand the concepts here, 90% of the battle is won.

{{ show_units(['introduction', 'sounds', 'verbs', 'nouns', 'uninflected-words', 'compounds']) }}

Part 2: Roots and sentences

Now that we have a strong foundation, we can focus on extremes. First, we zoom in on verb roots, the basic pieces of meaning that give us verb stems and thousands of other words. Then, we zoom out to sentences and the powerful words that help us maintain them.

Together, parts 1 and 2 provide us with enough fundamental concepts to start reading Sanskrit texts.

{{ show_units(['roots-and-classes', 'what-roots-can-do', 'pronouns', 'noun-stems']) }}
